## PRESENTATION DELIVERY TIPS

### Opening (First 10 seconds)
- **Confidence**: Stand center stage
- **Eye contact**: Look at judges, not slides
- **Volume**: Speak clearly, not too fast
- **Opening statement**: Deliver with passion (the "impossible choice" line)

### During Live Demo (2 minutes)
- **Narration**: Explain what's happening
- **Pauses**: Let important moments breathe
- **Emphasis**: Point to screen for key moments
- **Backup trigger**: At 30-second mark, if issues arise, switch to video

### Technical Slides (Slides 5-6)
- **Simplify**: Complex ideas in simple language
- **Analogies**: "Like WhatsApp encryption for supply chains"
- **Don't apologize**: Own the technical explanation
- **Emphasize benefit**: How does this help customers?

### Closing (Last 10 seconds)
- **Strong finish**: End with conviction
- **Call to action**: Clear ask or invitation
- **Thank judges**: Appreciation for their time
- **Open for Q&A**: Confident invitation for questions

### If Running Over Time
- Skip Slide 7 (Competitive Advantage) - least critical
- Shorten Slide 6 (Market) - reduce numbers, keep key points
- Brief technical explanation - reduce detail
- Quick closing - get to Q&A for engagement

### If Something Goes Wrong
- **Stay calm**: Audience won't notice if you don't react
- **Keep talking**: Use recovery lines (see DEMO_SCRIPT_DETAILED.md)
- **Acknowledge briefly**: "While that loads..." (don't dwell)
- **Move forward**: Get back into content quickly
- **Use backup**: At 1-minute mark, switch to video if needed
